# Trademark Policy

## The OpenBurn Brand

OpenBurn uses its own name, icon, and visual identity for this fork.

OpenUsage remains the upstream project brand owned by its maintainers. The MIT license covers the source code, not upstream branding.

## What You Can Do

- Use "OpenBurn" to refer to this fork
- Refer to OpenUsage only as the upstream project
- Say your project is compatible with OpenBurn when accurate

## What You Cannot Do

- Use OpenUsage as the name of this fork
- Use the OpenUsage logo as OpenBurn's logo
- Imply this fork is endorsed by, affiliated with, or an official part of OpenUsage
- Register OpenUsage-branded domains or social media accounts for this fork

## Forks

If you fork OpenBurn, you must:

1. Choose a different name for your fork
2. Remove the OpenBurn logo and branding unless you have permission
3. Clearly state that your project is a fork and not the official OpenBurn

## Questions

If you are unsure whether your use of the OpenBurn brand is allowed, open a discussion first.
